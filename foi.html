FOI Act 1982

Request your Information

Ever worried about the government stealing your information? The FOI Act of 1982 lets you request official data about you from government officials.

How to get your data.

To get your data from the requested individual, you have to provide a written letter to the office of the minister in question, and you will recieve your information later if it gets approved.

Exceptions to this

If the information you request poses a security to the nation for some reason, your information request will be denied and held by the government. (we don't want something bad happening)'

The FOI Act Webpage

Correcting Information under the FOI

You must submit a written request to the agency or minister in charge of your personal information, requesting that it be amended. When writing to the ministerial agency, include the following information: Identify the document(s) containing the personal information you want to change, if possible. provide a mailing address or an email address where the agency or minister can send you a letter or an email. Include a separate statement describing what personal information is missing, wrong, out of date, or misleading, as well as your grounds for making such a claim and any other information that would make your personal information complete, correct, up to date, or not misleading.
