What is Defamation?

What is the definition of defamation? Defamation is defined as a false and unprivileged statement of fact that is detrimental to a person's reputation and is published "with fault," which is due to negligence or malice. Defamation is often defined in precise ways by state legislation. Slander is a verbal defamation, while Libel is a written defamation.

How is Defamation used Online

Due to the dramatic rise of social media, it is easier than ever to send out something that can be detrimental to someones status, which is why defamation is a more relevant thing now than ever. With sites like twitter allowing users to be vocal about someone, truth or not and allowing them to "cancel" someone else, essentially ruining their reputation

How is Defamation balanced with Free Speech

Defamation is defined as a communication from one person to at least one other that is intended to injure the reputation of an identified third party and for which the communicator (the publisher) has no legal defence. The goal of defamation law is to strike a balance between the right to free speech and the protection of a person's reputation from injury.

How is Defamation Proven in Court

If you have been defamed and wish to consult a lawyer and take it to court, there are three things to take into account. In order for a defamation lawsuit to proceed, the person who has been defamed (the plaintiff) must show three things: The communication has been published to a third party; it identifies (or is about) the plaintiff; and it is defamatory.
